ABOUT EMRE

I am a PhD student under the supervision of Dr. Keith Moored in the department of Mechanical Engineering and Mechanics at Lehigh University. I am working on understanding the physics of fast speed high efficiency fish swimming. I have primarily worked on intermittent gaits and the conditions that make intermittent motion superior to continuous motion. Prior to joining the biofluids research lab, I graduated from Middle East Thecnical University with a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering. Additionally, I spent a semester abroad in TU Delft in the Netherlands.

I am curious about various things but for the last few years I started to become interested in human psychology, various ways of manipulating and expressing emotions and feelings. Currently, I am building a pipeline to predict the emotional state of marathon runners based on their pictures. While running long distance, you clear your mind from day-to-day psychological stressors, whereas, on the other hand, you load a more fundamental physiological stress to your body. So, I found observing and predicting this trade-off particularly interesting.
