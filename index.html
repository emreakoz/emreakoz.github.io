ABOUT EMRE

I have been fascinated by the weather since I was a child. This fascination brought me to Penn State, where I received a BS and MS in Meteorology and Atmospheric Science in August 2018.

During my education, I developed a strong analytical background through work on my MS thesis, research at the NASA Goddard Institute for Space Studies, research at the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) Earth System Research Laboratory, and through field-based courses I took in the high Arctic. In these experiences, I evaluated climate models, analyzed large and diverse observational datasets, and learned how to use numerical models to study atmospheric processes. I also developed a strong background in scientific communication to both technical and non-technical audiences.

I am currently building a background in catastrophe risk, statistical modeling, and machine learning through independent study and personal projects. Links to these projects are provided below.
